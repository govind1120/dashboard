Socio-economic Data

Performance Linked Enhancement of Academic Studies and Endeavour (PLEASE) project proposes to setup practices and framework for releasing public data for the state of Kerala. The proposed project aims to study and put in place practices to curate public data and convert such data into highly accessible and machine- friendly linked open data formats.

Socioeconomic data refers to information that describes the economic and social characteristics of a population, such as income, education level, employment status, occupation, and household composition. It can be collected through a variety of sources, including surveys, census data, and administrative records. Here, focus area of socio-economic data includes: Technology, employment and labour market data, innovation survey in agriculture, data on the social capital network and local governance and data on environmental issues with specific to solid waste management.

Technology, employment and labour market data mainly focus on firm level data on technology, employment and wage structure in the unorganized labour market and also the presence of modern technology in the work place. Collecting firm- level data on technology, employment, and wage structure can provide valuable insights into the labor market and help identify trends and challenges that workers and employers face.

Analyzing these types of data can help policymakers and researchers identify areas where workers may need additional support or training, as well as areas where firms may need incentives to invest in new technologies or create new jobs. By understanding the dynamics of the labor market and the impact of technology on employment and wages, policymakers can develop policies and programs that support workers and promote economic growth.

Innovation survey in agriculture data focus on data about the various technologies invented in the agriculture like introduction of wide variety of seeds, irrigation facilities, tractors, tillers, etc. We mainly included APY statistics (Area, Production and productivity) of various agricultural crops such as rice, wheat, tea, rubber, cardamom, coffee, pepper etc of Kerala & India and district wise statistics of Area, Production, Productivity (APY) of rice, black pepper, cardamom, banana, cashew nut, cotton (lint), and turmeric of Kerala. Along with this, data on raw materials for agriculture such as wide variety of seeds, irrigation facilities, tractors, tillers, rainfall etc. are included in the data portal. Collecting data on innovation in agriculture can provide important insights into the ways in which new technologies and practices are transforming the sector. We are providing agriculture data from reliable open sources such as Economic Review, EPWRF (Economic and Political Weekly Research Foundation), Meteorological Department, DES (Directorate of Economics & Statistics), Indiastat, Rubber Board.

By collecting and analyzing this type of data, policymakers and researchers can gain a deeper understanding of the challenges and opportunities facing the agricultural sector. This can help to frame policies and programs that support the adoption of new technologies, improve productivity, and promote sustainable and equitable growth in agriculture.

Solid waste management mainly focuses on data on household solid waste generation, involvement of public- private participation and data on the employment opportunity and empowerment of economically and socially disadvantaged groups in waste management. It provides data on solid waste generation, collection, treatment, land filled in India and Kerala. Along with this, it includes data about state wise plastic waste generation and sewage generation. Here, the study presents data on solid waste management from reliable open sources such as Central Pollution Control Board, State Pollution Control Board, Economic Review, Indiastat etc.

About Us

This project proposes to set up practices and framework for releasing public data in the Linked Open Data for the State of Kerala. In this regard, we propose to establish an Open Data Laboratory at Government College Kasaragod. Further, the lab will function as a nodal centre for training students of science, humanities and engineering for data curation, analysis and releases. Further, the project envisages multidisciplinary collaboration among faculty members in India and abroad to work towards open data practices and assist interested government departments and industries to convert their data into Linked Open Data (LOD) format.

Labour Data

Labour data consists of a comprehensive, multi-dimensional body of official statistics regarding work, covering both the supply of labour—such as population, employment, unemployment, and labor force participation rates—and the demand for it, including vacancy rates, industrial relations, and working conditions. Collected through household surveys (like the PLFS in India), population censuses, and establishment surveys, this data is crucial for analyzing labor productivity, wage structures, and the shift between formal and informal sectors. These metrics allow policymakers, researchers, and international organizations like the ILO to monitor the health of the economy, evaluate the quality of employment, and develop evidence-based policies for economic growth and social protection.

About Us

This project proposes to set up practices and framework for releasing public data in the Linked Open Data for the State of Kerala. In this regard, we propose to establish an Open Data Laboratory at Government College Kasaragod. Further, the lab will function as a nodal centre for training students of science, humanities and engineering for data curation, analysis and releases. Further, the project envisages multidisciplinary collaboration among faculty members in India and abroad to work towards open data practices and assist interested government departments and industries to convert their data into Linked Open Data (LOD) format.

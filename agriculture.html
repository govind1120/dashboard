Agriculture Datasets

Agriculture is the backbone of the world's food supply, providing the food and resources needed to support a growing population. Agriculture has evolved over the years, from traditional subsistence farming to modern commercial agriculture that utilizes advanced technologies and methods such as seeds, fertilizers, pesticides and machinery to increase crop yields and improve efficiency. In addition to food production, agriculture also plays an important role in the economy, providing employment and income for millions of people worldwide.

Access to accurate and reliable agricultural data is essential for making informed decisions and policies related to food security, rural development, and sustainable agriculture. Through the Performance Linked Enhancement of Academic Studies and Endeavour (PLEASE) project, we provide APY data (Area, Production and Productivity) of various agricultural crops such as rice, wheat, tea, rubber, cardamom, coffee, pepper etc. In addition to providing APY data, it is also important to consider other factors that impact agricultural production, such as climate change, soil health, water availability, and pest and disease management. By analyzing and understanding these factors, policymakers and farmers can adopt sustainable and climate-resilient agricultural practices that can improve yields, reduce production costs, and enhance environmental sustainability. Overall, it is encouraging to see efforts to gather and share agricultural data to support evidence- based decision-making and promote sustainable agriculture. By analyzing APY data for different crops, stakeholders can also identify areas where improvements in technology, irrigation, fertilizers, and pest control could help to increase yields and improve productivity. This information can be particularly useful for smallholder farmers who often face challenges in accessing the resources needed to optimize crop production.

Moreover, APY data can help to identify potential food security challenges and guide interventions aimed at improving agricultural resilience and reducing vulnerability to shocks such as climate change, natural disasters, or market fluctuations. Overall, the provision of APY data for various agricultural crops is a valuable contribution to the agricultural sector, and can help to support sustainable development and economic growth.

About Us

This project proposes to set up practices and framework for releasing public data in the Linked Open Data for the State of Kerala. In this regard, we propose to establish an Open Data Laboratory at Government College Kasaragod. Further, the lab will function as a nodal centre for training students of science, humanities and engineering for data curation, analysis and releases. Further, the project envisages multidisciplinary collaboration among faculty members in India and abroad to work towards open data practices and assist interested government departments and industries to convert their data into Linked Open Data (LOD) format.

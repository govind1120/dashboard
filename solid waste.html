Solid Waste Management Data

Solid waste management refers to the process of collection, treatment, and disposal of solid waste in a safe and sustainable manner. Solid waste, also known as garbage, includes any discarded material that is not liquid or gas. Examples of solid waste include household waste, commercial waste, industrial waste, construction and demolition waste, and hazardous waste. Sustainable solid waste management practices prioritize waste reduction, reuse, and recycling to minimize the amount of waste sent to landfills or incineration. This can involve implementing waste reduction programs, such as composting and recycling initiatives.Performance Linked Enhancement of Academic Studies and Endeavour (PLEASE) project is providing valuable data on solid waste generation and management in India and Kerala. Having reliable and up-to-date information on solid waste generation, collection, treatment, and disposal is essential for developing effective waste management strategies and policies. By monitoring and optimizing their waste management processes, municipalities and waste treatment plants can improve their efficiency and reduce their environmental impact. This can also help to reduce the amount of waste sent to landfills or incineration, which can have significant environmental and health consequences.Furthermore, the data on plastic waste generation and sewage is also important, as these are major contributors to environmental pollution. By tracking these trends, policymakers and researchers can develop targeted interventions to reduce plastic waste and improve sewage treatment. Overall, it has the potential to inform evidence-based policies and interventions that can improve the sustainability and environmental health of India and Kerala.

About Us

This project proposes to set up practices and framework for releasing public data in the Linked Open Data for the State of Kerala. In this regard, we propose to establish an Open Data Laboratory at Government College Kasaragod. Further, the lab will function as a nodal centre for training students of science, humanities and engineering for data curation, analysis and releases. Further, the project envisages multidisciplinary collaboration among faculty members in India and abroad to work towards open data practices and assist interested government departments and industries to convert their data into Linked Open Data (LOD) format.
